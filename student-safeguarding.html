Safeguarding

Your Safety is Our Priority

If you are in immediate danger or need urgent help, please contact emergency services or use the Emergency Support button above.

Remember: Stay SAFE

S
Speak up - Tell a trusted adult if something doesn't feel right or if you're worried about yourself or someone else.
A
Ask for help - It's okay to ask for support. There are always people ready to listen and help you.
F
Find a trusted adult - Identify adults you can talk to, like teachers, counselors, family members, or friends.
E
Everyone deserves to be safe - You have the right to feel safe and protected at all times.

Who Can You Talk To?

Your Teachers

All teachers are trained to listen and help. You can talk to any teacher you feel comfortable with.

School Counselor

Our trained counselors are here to provide support and guidance in a safe, confidential environment.

Family & Friends

Trusted family members and friends can provide support and help you find the right resources.

Helplines

Anonymous support available 24/7. See contact details below.

Safeguarding Team

Our dedicated safeguarding officers are specially trained to handle sensitive situations.

Online Support

Access online chat services and digital support platforms available 24/7.

What is Safeguarding?

  • Protecting you from harm and abuse
  • Ensuring your physical and emotional wellbeing
  • Creating a safe learning environment
  • Supporting you when you need help
  • Respecting your rights and dignity

Types of Concerns

  • Physical, emotional, or sexual abuse
  • Neglect or abandonment
  • Bullying or cyberbullying
  • Discrimination or harassment
  • Mental health concerns
  • Substance abuse
  • Online safety issues

Your Rights

  • Right to feel safe and protected
  • Right to be heard and believed
  • Right to confidential support
  • Right to be treated with respect
  • Right to access help and resources
  • Right to privacy (within safety limits)
